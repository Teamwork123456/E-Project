Dr. Sarah Johnson

Professor of Artificial Intelligence | PhD Stanford University

Years Teaching

18

Publications

24

Awards

7

Dr. Johnson joined COURSE College in 2010 after completing her postdoctoral research at MIT's Computer Science and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory. Her groundbreaking work on explainable AI systems has helped bridge the gap between complex machine learning models and human interpretability.

Before entering academia, Dr. Johnson worked as a lead researcher at DeepMind, where she contributed to projects that advanced natural language processing capabilities. She brings this industry experience into the classroom, ensuring students understand both the theoretical foundations and practical applications of AI.

In 2022, Dr. Johnson was awarded the prestigious Turing Fellowship for her contributions to ethical AI development. She currently leads the college's Responsible AI Initiative, which brings together students, faculty, and industry partners to address challenges in algorithmic fairness and transparency.

Outside the classroom, Dr. Johnson enjoys mentoring undergraduate research projects and has supervised over 50 student publications in top-tier conferences. Her teaching philosophy emphasizes hands-on learning, and she regularly incorporates real-world datasets and problems into her coursework.

Prof. Michael Chen

Head of Software Engineering | MS Carnegie Mellon

Years in Industry

15

Patents

5

Startups Founded

2

Professor Chen brings a wealth of practical experience to his teaching, having served as Principal Engineer at TechCorp where he led the development of their flagship cloud platform serving over 10 million users worldwide. His transition to academia in 2018 was driven by a passion for educating the next generation of software architects.

At COURSE College, Prof. Chen developed the innovative "Software Studio" course where student teams work on semester-long projects for real clients. This program has resulted in several student-founded startups and has been recognized by the ACM for excellence in engineering education.

His research focuses on distributed systems reliability, and he regularly consults for major tech companies on scaling challenges. Prof. Chen is known for his engaging teaching style that combines war stories from Silicon Valley with rigorous computer science fundamentals.

Beyond the classroom, Prof. Chen serves as faculty advisor to the college's hackathon team, which has won multiple national competitions under his guidance. He also leads industry networking events that have helped over 200 students secure internships at top tech firms.
